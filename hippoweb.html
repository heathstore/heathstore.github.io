All About Hippos

Introduction

A hippos is also known as a hippopotamus. Hippos in the wild can live up to 50 years. Hippos love water. They spend up to 16 hours in the water. There are two species of hippos that live on Earth today, the Common Hippopotamus and the Pygmy Hippopotamus. Some people believe that hippos are descendants of pigs but recent studies show that they are related to dolphins and whales.

Where Hippos Live

Wild hippos live in the continent known as Africa. Common Hippos usually near to rivers and lakes south of the Sahara Desert. Pygmy Hippos are usually found in swampy forest of western Africa. The Common Hippopotamus usually live in groups called pods. On the other hand Pygmy Hippopotamus prefer to live alone.

What Hippos Eat

Hippos mostly eat grass and a little bit of aquatic plants. This means hippos are herbivores. Hippos usually eat at night and graze 1 to 2 miles of grass for 4 to 5 hours. Intestines of baby hippos require their mother's feces to digest vegetation.

Endangered And Extinct Hippos

The Pygmy Hippopotamus are soon to be extinct. Their habitat, swampy forests, are being cut down. On top of that Pygmy Hippopotamus like to have their personal territory. There is in total five extinct hippos. Three Malagasy species became extinct during the Holocene on Madagascar. Two species of the European hippopotamus became extinct before the last glaciation. There is now less than 125,000 hippos left in the wild. they are being hunted because of their meat and canine teeth that are being used for ivory.

Physical Traits for Common Hippo

Common Hippos hold the record for the second largest mammals in the world after the elephant. The male Common Hippo usually weighs more than 6,000 pounds and the female weighs 4,000 pounds. Pygmy Hippos weigh almost the same but are only half as tall. Hippos eyes and ears are located high on their head. Even though they look like chubby, cuddly, cute animals, they can easily outrun a human.
